# RIA Structure Comparison: Belgian Federal Template vs. EU Impact Assessment

## Overview

This document compares the structure of two different RIA (Regulatory Impact Assessment) formats:
1. **Belgian Federal RIA Template** (RIA_example.docx) - Standardized government form
2. **EU Commission Impact Assessment** (EU Impact Assessments_SWD(2022)167_main_2_EN.pdf) - Comprehensive assessment document

---

## Structure Comparison

### Belgian Federal RIA Template Structure

#### **Format**: Standardized Form/Checklist

#### **Main Sections**:

1. **Descriptive Sheet**
   - **A. Author**
     - Competent government member
     - Policy cell contact person
     - Public service contact person
   - **B. Draft**
     - Title of the regulation
     - Short description
     - Origin (treaty, directive, cooperation agreement, current events)
     - Intended implementation goals
     - Impact analyses already carried out (Yes/No)
   - **C. Consultation on the draft regulation**
     - Mandatory, optional, or informal consultations
   - **D. Sources used to carry out the impact analysis**
     - Statistics, reference documents, organizations, reference persons

2. **21 Impact Themes Assessment**
   - Each theme assessed with:
     - ☒ Positive impact / ☐ Negative impact / ☐ No impact
     - Explanation (brief text)
     - Additional questions for specific themes (e.g., gender composition, SME involvement)

3. **Administrative Burdens Section** (Theme 11)
   - Detailed breakdown:
     - Target groups involved
     - Formalities/obligations (current vs. draft regulation)
     - Documents and information required
     - Collection methods
     - Periodicity
     - Mitigation measures

#### **21 Impact Themes**:
1. Combating poverty
2. Equal opportunities and social cohesion
3. Equality between women and men
4. Health
5. Employment
6. Consumption and production patterns
7. Economic development
8. Investments
9. Research and development
10. SMEs
11. Administrative burdens
12. Energy
13. Mobility
14. Food
15. Climate change
16. Natural resources
17. Outdoor and indoor air
18. Biodiversity
19. Nuisance
20. Government
21. Policy coherence for development

### EU Commission Impact Assessment Structure

#### **Format**: Comprehensive Analytical Document

#### **Main Sections**:

1. **Executive Summary / Main Document**
   - Problem definition
   - Objectives
   - Policy options
   - Impact assessment
   - Comparison of options
   - Subsidiarity and proportionality
   - Monitoring and evaluation

2. **Annexes** (12 annexes):
   - **Annex I**: Procedural information
   - **Annex II**: Stakeholder consultation
   - **Annex III**: Who is affected and how?
   - **Annex IV**: Analytical methods
   - **Annex V**: Specific targets considered for main ecosystem types
   - **Annex VI**: Analysis by ecosystem
   - **Annex VII**: Description, trends and impacts of main options
   - **Annex VIII**: Background information for potential restoration targets
   - **Annex IX**: Evaluation of EU Biodiversity Strategy to 2020
   - **Annex X**: Coherence with EU legislation and policy initiatives
   - **Annex XI**: Restoration frameworks in Member States
   - **Annex XII**: Financing options at EU level

3. **Ten Thematic Areas** (Ecosystem-Based):
   1. Agro-ecosystems
   2. Inland wetlands
   3. Coastal wetlands
   4. Marine ecosystems
   5. Forests
   6. Rivers, lakes and alluvial habitats
   7. Steppe, heath and scrub
   8. Urban ecosystems
   9. Soil ecosystems (cross-cutting)
   10. Pollinators (cross-cutting)

4. **Impact Dimensions Assessed**:
   - Economic (costs, benefits, opportunity costs)
   - Social (employment, health, cultural services)
   - Environmental (biodiversity, climate, ecosystem services)
   - Administrative (enabling measures, monitoring)
   - Stakeholder-specific (farmers, fishers, nature managers, etc.)
   - Geographic (Member State variations)
   - Temporal (short, medium, long-term)

## Key Differences

### 1. **Purpose and Scope**

**Belgian Federal Template**:
- **Purpose**: Standardized checklist for all regulatory proposals
- **Scope**: Broad coverage of 21 predefined themes
- **Format**: Form-based, checkbox-driven
- **Length**: Concise (typically 5-10 pages)
- **Audience**: Government decision-makers, public

**EU Impact Assessment**:
- **Purpose**: Comprehensive analysis for major policy initiatives
- **Scope**: Deep dive into specific policy area (nature restoration)
- **Format**: Analytical document with extensive evidence
- **Length**: Extensive (hundreds of pages with annexes)
- **Audience**: EU institutions, Member States, stakeholders, public

### 2. **Assessment Methodology**

**Belgian Federal Template**:
- **Method**: Qualitative assessment (Positive/Negative/No impact)
- **Evidence**: Brief explanations, references to sources
- **Quantification**: Minimal (mostly qualitative)
- **Depth**: Surface-level assessment per theme
- **Standardization**: High (same format for all proposals)

**EU Impact Assessment**:
- **Method**: Mixed qualitative and quantitative analysis
- **Evidence**: Extensive data, studies, modeling, expert input
- **Quantification**: Detailed cost-benefit analysis with monetary estimates
- **Depth**: Deep analysis with multiple dimensions
- **Standardization**: Moderate (adapts to policy area)

### 3. **Theme Structure**

**Belgian Federal Template**:
- **21 Fixed Themes**: Same themes for every proposal
- **Assessment**: Simple checkbox (Positive/Negative/No impact)
- **Flexibility**: Low (must assess all 21 themes)
- **Relevance**: Some themes may not be relevant to all proposals

**EU Impact Assessment**:
- **Thematic Areas**: Policy-specific (ecosystem types in this case)
- **Assessment**: Multi-dimensional (costs, benefits, feasibility, etc.)
- **Flexibility**: High (adapts to policy area)
- **Relevance**: All areas directly relevant to the policy

### 4. **Administrative Burdens**

**Belgian Federal Template**:
- **Dedicated Theme**: Administrative burdens is Theme #11
- **Structure**: Detailed breakdown:
  - Current vs. draft regulation comparison
  - Formalities, documents, collection methods
  - Periodicity
  - Mitigation measures
- **Focus**: Compliance burden on citizens/enterprises

**EU Impact Assessment**:
- **Integrated Approach**: Administrative costs included in overall cost analysis
- **Structure**: Part of enabling measures section
- **Focus**: Implementation costs for Member States and businesses
- **Quantification**: Detailed cost estimates

### 5. **Stakeholder Analysis**

**Belgian Federal Template**:
- **Approach**: Implicit (through theme assessments)
- **Explicit Stakeholder Sections**: Limited
- **Gender Analysis**: Explicit (Theme 3)
- **SME Analysis**: Explicit (Theme 10)

**EU Impact Assessment**:
- **Approach**: Explicit stakeholder analysis
- **Stakeholder Sections**: Dedicated sections (Annex III)
- **Stakeholder Groups**: Detailed breakdown (farmers, fishers, nature managers, etc.)
- **Distributional Analysis**: Member State and regional variations

### 6. **Evidence and Sources**

**Belgian Federal Template**:
- **Sources Section**: Simple list (Section D)
- **Evidence Depth**: Minimal (brief explanations)
- **References**: Limited citations
- **Data**: Mostly qualitative

**EU Impact Assessment**:
- **Sources**: Extensive references throughout
- **Evidence Depth**: Comprehensive (multiple studies, data sources)
- **References**: Detailed citations and footnotes
- **Data**: Quantitative and qualitative, with modeling

### 7. **Cost-Benefit Analysis**

**Belgian Federal Template**:
- **Approach**: Qualitative (positive/negative impact)
- **Quantification**: Minimal
- **Monetary Estimates**: Not required
- **Time Horizon**: Not specified

**EU Impact Assessment**:
- **Approach**: Quantitative cost-benefit analysis
- **Quantification**: Detailed monetary estimates
- **Monetary Estimates**: Present value calculations (2022-2070)
- **Time Horizon**: Explicit (2030, 2040, 2050, 2070)
- **Discount Rate**: 4% social discount rate

### 8. **Options Analysis**

**Belgian Federal Template**:
- **Options**: Single proposal assessed
- **Comparison**: Not required
- **Alternatives**: Not explicitly considered

**EU Impact Assessment**:
- **Options**: Multiple policy options assessed
- **Comparison**: Detailed comparison of options
- **Alternatives**: Baseline, Option 2, Option 3, Option 4 (preferred)
- **Selection Criteria**: Effectiveness, efficiency, feasibility

### 2. **Impact Dimensions**

- Administrative impacts

### 3. **Stakeholder Consideration**

## Implications for RIA System Design

### For Belgian Federal Template Format:

**Required Output Structure**:
1. **Descriptive Sheet** (A, B, C, D)
2. **21 Theme Assessments**:
   - Impact type (Positive/Negative/No impact)
   - Explanation for each theme
   - Additional questions for specific themes
3. **Administrative Burdens** (detailed breakdown)

**Key Challenges**:
- Must assess all 21 themes (even if not relevant)
- Brief explanations required
- Checkbox format (structured output needed)
- Some themes require additional structured data (gender, SMEs)

**LLM Council Adaptation**:
- Generate impact assessment for each of 21 themes
- Classify impact type (Positive/Negative/No impact)
- Generate brief explanations
- Extract structured data for special themes
- Format as checkbox form

### For EU Impact Assessment Format:

**Required Output Structure**:
1. **Main Document** (problem, objectives, options, impacts)
2. **Annexes** (procedural, stakeholder, analytical methods, etc.)
3. **Thematic Assessments** (policy-specific, e.g., ecosystems)
4. **Cost-Benefit Analysis** (quantitative)
5. **Stakeholder Analysis** (detailed)

**Key Challenges**:
- Policy-specific thematic areas (not fixed)
- Extensive quantitative analysis
- Multiple options comparison
- Deep evidence base required

**LLM Council Adaptation**:
- Identify relevant thematic areas for policy
- Generate comprehensive analysis per area
- Provide quantitative estimates where possible
- Compare multiple options
- Reference evidence and sources

## Recommended Approach for RIA System

### Support Both Formats

**1. Belgian Federal Template (Primary Format)**
- **Use Case**: Standard government RIA submissions
- **Structure**: Fixed 21-theme format
- **Output**: Form-based, checkbox format
- **Complexity**: Lower (structured, standardized)

**2. EU Impact Assessment (Secondary Format)**
- **Use Case**: Comprehensive policy analysis
- **Structure**: Flexible, policy-specific
- **Output**: Analytical document with annexes
- **Complexity**: Higher (requires deeper analysis)

### Implementation Strategy

**For Belgian Format**:
- **Template**: Fixed structure with 21 themes
- **LLM Prompting**: 
  - Assess each theme systematically
  - Classify impact type
  - Generate brief explanations
  - Extract structured data for special themes
- **Output Formatting**: Match exact form structure

**For EU Format**:
- **Template**: Flexible structure based on policy area
- **LLM Prompting**:
  - Identify relevant thematic areas
  - Generate comprehensive analysis
  - Provide quantitative estimates
  - Compare options
- **Output Formatting**: Professional document with annexes

### Hybrid Approach

**Best of Both Worlds**:
- Use Belgian 21-theme structure as **base framework**
- Add **EU-style depth** where relevant:
  - Quantitative analysis for economic themes
  - Detailed stakeholder analysis
  - Cost-benefit analysis for major impacts
  - Evidence base and references

**Enhanced Belgian Format**:
- Keep 21-theme structure (standardization)
- Add quantitative analysis where possible
- Include detailed administrative burden analysis
- Add stakeholder breakdown
- Include evidence references

## Summary

### Belgian Federal Template
- ✅ **Standardized**: Same format for all proposals
- ✅ **Comprehensive**: Covers 21 predefined themes
- ✅ **Simple**: Easy to complete and review
- ⚠️ **Rigid**: Must assess all themes (even if not relevant)
- ⚠️ **Shallow**: Brief explanations only

### EU Impact Assessment
- ✅ **Comprehensive**: Deep analysis with evidence
- ✅ **Flexible**: Adapts to policy area
- ✅ **Quantitative**: Detailed cost-benefit analysis
- ⚠️ **Complex**: Requires extensive resources
- ⚠️ **Time-consuming**: Long development process

### Recommendation

**For the RIA System**:
- **Primary Format**: Belgian Federal Template (21 themes)
- **Enhancement**: Add EU-style depth where relevant
- **Flexibility**: Support both formats
- **LLM Adaptation**: 
  - Generate 21-theme assessments
  - Add quantitative analysis for relevant themes
  - Include detailed administrative burden analysis
  - Provide evidence references

This approach provides:
- **Standardization** (21-theme structure)
- **Comprehensiveness** (all relevant impacts covered)
- **Depth** (where needed)
- **Efficiency** (automated generation)
- **Quality** (LLM Council ensures robust assessments)
